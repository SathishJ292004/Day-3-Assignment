LPG News

India is currently facing concerns about an LPG cylinder shortage and rising prices due to global fuel supply disruptions. The situation has been influenced by tensions in the Middle East and the Strait of Hormuz, which are key routes for global oil and gas shipments. Many cities in India have reported shortages of commercial LPG cylinders, affecting restaurants, street food vendors, hostels, and catering businesses. Some eateries have reduced menu items or temporarily stopped operations because of limited cooking gas supply. In several areas, long queues have formed at gas agencies and fuel stations as people try to stock up on LPG amid fears of further shortages. The Indian government has stated that domestic LPG supply for households remains stable and has increased refinery production and imports from countries such as the United States and Norway to manage the situation.

Recently, LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas) prices in India have increased, affecting both households and businesses. Oil marketing companies raised the price of domestic LPG cylinders by about ₹60 and commercial cylinders by about ₹115 across major cities. The increase is mainly due to rising global energy prices and tensions in the Middle East, which have disrupted fuel supply routes and increased import costs. Because of the shortage of commercial cylinders, some small businesses such as restaurants, tea stalls, and street food vendors are facing difficulties and increasing food prices.
